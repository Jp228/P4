All you need to know about Cricket!

Bowl the ball

The ball is to be bowled. The bowler begins behind the bowling crease and bowls the ball before going forward and tossing the ball to the popping crease. A cricket bowl is often thrown over the shoulder with the arm perfectly straight. It is permissible for the ball to bounce once on the pitch before hitting the striker, but it is not necessary.


Hit the ball and run

The striker will try to smash the ball with the flat side of the cricket bat. There are several different types of strikes, each with its own set of advantages. After the striker has struck the ball, the non-striker and striker will alternate running from end to end of the pitch. If both batsmen reach the far side of the pitch safely, one run is declared and one point is scored. No point is scored if any batsman is ruled out when attempting to hit the far side of the ground.


Finish the game

Play according to whatever type of game you've chosen until the appropriate number of innings has been reached. The team with the highest scores of runs is the winner.
